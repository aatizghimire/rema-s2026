# 📘 Frequently Asked Questions

### **1. Who can enroll in this course?**
The course is designed for students and professionals interested in cybersecurity. Students should have basic Windows familiarity, an understanding of fundamental programming concepts (variables, functions, loops in any language), and the ability to install and configure software on a virtual machine. No prior reverse engineering or malware analysis experience is required.

### **2. How can I register for the course?**
Registrations are not yet open. Once they are, you will be notified via email from the administration or department office. 

After registration opens, you can register by clicking the “Register” button on the course portal.

### **3. Is there an interview process?**
Depending on the number of applications received, we may initiate a short screening process to ensure you have the appropriate system hardware to run Flare-VM and the commitment required for this 10-week intensive bootcamp.

### **4. Will students receive credits for this course?**
This is an intensive enrichment bootcamp and currently does not carry academic credit. However, it complements core cybersecurity modules and drastically enhances employability through practical, hands-on exposure to real-world threats.

### **5. What tools and programming languages will be used?**
Students will gain hands-on experience entirely with free and open-source software:

- **Core Tools**: x64dbg, Ghidra, Oracle VirtualBox, Flare-VM, Scylla, PE-Bear, Process Hacker 
- **Analysis Tools**: ProcMon, RegShot, Wireshark, Detect It Easy (DiE), YARA
- **Languages/Concepts**: x86 Assembly, understanding of C/C++ structures, and basic scripting.

### **6. Will there be any certification?**
Yes. Students completing the final capstone project, all assignments, and maintaining attendance will receive a **Certificate of Completion in Reverse Engineering & Malware Analysis** demonstrating proficiency in debugging, unpacking, and static analysis.

### **7. What are the expected outcomes?**
Participants will acquire a robust understanding of malicious software behavior, manual unpacking techniques, memory dumping, and deep static analysis—skills critical for roles in Incident Response, Threat Intelligence, and Malware Analysis.

### **8. Are group projects allowed?**
Weekly lab exercises and homework are individual to ensure fundamental skill development. However, the final Capstone Malware Analysis report may be undertaken individually or in pairs, depending on the complexity of the provided sample.

### **9. Where can I access course materials?**
All course slides, lab instructions, and submission portals will be made available via the course repository. Encrypted, safely packaged malware samples and the Flare-VM setup guides will be provided to enrolled students prior to Week 1.

### **10. Will there be Assignments?**
Yes. There are 9 weekly homework assignments requiring 3-5 hours of commitment. However, if you complete the practical analysis tasks during the 2-hour live workshops, your take-home workload will be significantly reduced. 

### **11. Is attendance mandatory?**
Yes. Attendance is **mandatory** for all live workshops. Due to the complex, hands-on nature of debugging and unpacking, active participation is essential and contributes 10% toward your final grade. 

### **12. Can I withdraw from the course after enrolling?**
Withdrawals are **only permitted before the first official class session (Week 1)**. After this period, enrollment is locked, and the student is expected to complete all coursework and the final capstone project.
